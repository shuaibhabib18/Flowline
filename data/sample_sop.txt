STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Client Identity Verification — FINTRAC Compliance
================================================================================

SOP ID:         WS-KYC-001
Version:        2.1
Effective Date: January 15, 2026
Department:     Compliance & Operations
Approved By:    Chief Compliance Officer
Review Cycle:   Annually (next review: January 2027)
Classification: Internal — Confidential

================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

This procedure establishes the steps required to verify the identity of new
clients opening accounts with Wealthsimple, in compliance with the Proceeds of
Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act (PCMLTFA) and FINTRAC
guidelines. All reporting entities registered as securities dealers must verify
client identity before opening an account or conducting transactions.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE
================================================================================

This SOP applies to all Wealthsimple employees, agents, and automated systems
involved in the client onboarding process, including:
- Client-facing application intake
- Identity verification operations
- Compliance review and escalation
- Record keeping and audit trail

================================================================================
3. PROCEDURE
================================================================================

STEP 1: Receive New Client Application
---------------------------------------
Owner: System (Automated)
When a new client submits an account opening application through the
Wealthsimple app or website, the system automatically captures:
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Citizenship / country of residence
- Occupation and employer
- Purpose of the account
- Source of funds declaration

The system validates that all mandatory fields are completed and stores the
application in the onboarding queue.

STEP 2: Screen Against Sanctions and PEP Lists
-----------------------------------------------
Owner: System (Automated)
The system automatically screens the client against:
- Canadian sanctions lists (OSFI consolidated list)
- UN Security Council sanctions lists
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) databases — both domestic and foreign
- Heads of International Organizations (HIO) lists
- FINTRAC advisories and intelligence reports

The screening must be completed before any account is opened. Results are logged
with timestamp, source database version, and match confidence scores.

STEP 3: Assess Client Risk Level
---------------------------------
Owner: System (Automated)
Based on the screening results and client profile, the system assigns an initial
risk rating:

HIGH RISK indicators:
- PEP or HIO match (domestic or foreign)
- Sanctions list partial match
- Client from high-risk jurisdiction (as per FATF grey/black list)
- Unusual source of funds (e.g., cash-intensive business)
- Client is a trust, nominee, or legal arrangement

STANDARD RISK:
- No adverse screening results
- Canadian resident with standard employment
- Common account types (TFSA, RRSP, non-registered)

DECISION: Is the client flagged as high risk?
- If YES → proceed to Step 4 (Enhanced Due Diligence)
- If NO → proceed to Step 5 (Standard Identity Verification)

STEP 4: Perform Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
---------------------------------------------
Owner: Compliance Officer (Human)
For high-risk clients, a compliance officer must:
a) Review the screening results and risk assessment manually
b) Obtain additional information about the client's source of funds and source
   of wealth
c) Determine the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship
d) Search public databases, news sources, and the internet for adverse
   information about the client
e) Document the rationale for proceeding or declining the application
f) Obtain approval from the Senior Compliance Officer before proceeding

If the compliance officer determines the risk is unacceptable, the application
is declined and the client is notified. A record of the decision and rationale
must be kept for at least 5 years.

After completing EDD, proceed to Step 5.

STEP 5: Verify Client Identity — Select Method
------------------------------------------------
Owner: System / Compliance Officer
The client's identity must be verified using one of the following FINTRAC-
approved methods. The method selection depends on whether the client is
onboarding digitally or in-person:

DIGITAL ONBOARDING (primary method for Wealthsimple):
→ Use the Government-Issued Photo ID Method (Step 6)

ALTERNATIVE METHODS (if photo ID method is not feasible):
→ Credit File Method — verify identity using Canadian credit bureau data
→ Dual-Process Method — use two independent sources from different categories

DECISION: Which verification method is being used?
- Government Photo ID → proceed to Step 6
- Credit File → proceed to Step 7
- Dual Process → proceed to Step 8

STEP 6: Government-Issued Photo Identification Method
------------------------------------------------------
Owner: System + Compliance Officer (Hybrid)
This is the primary verification method for digital onboarding:

a) The client uploads or presents a valid, original, government-issued photo
   identification document. Acceptable documents include:
   - Canadian passport
   - Canadian driver's licence (provincial/territorial)
   - Canadian permanent resident card
   - Provincial/territorial health card (where permitted for ID purposes)
   - Canadian citizenship card (issued before 2012 with photo)
   - Foreign passport

b) The system uses automated document verification to:
   - Confirm the document is authentic (not altered or forged)
   - Extract the client's name, date of birth, and photo
   - Perform liveness detection to confirm the client is a real person
   - Match the photo on the document to the client's selfie

c) A compliance officer reviews the automated verification results and confirms:
   - The name on the document matches the application
   - The document is not expired
   - The photo matches the client
   - There are no signs of tampering or forgery

d) Record the following information:
   - Type of document
   - Document number
   - Issuing jurisdiction
   - Expiry date
   - Date of verification
   - Name of the person who verified the identity

DECISION: Was the identity successfully verified?
- If YES → proceed to Step 9 (Create Compliance Record)
- If NO → proceed to Step 10 (Escalate to Senior Compliance)

STEP 7: Credit File Method
---------------------------
Owner: System (Automated)
This method verifies identity by matching client information against Canadian
credit bureau records:

a) The system queries a Canadian credit bureau (Equifax or TransUnion)
b) The credit file must have existed for at least 3 years
c) The system verifies that the following information matches:
   - Client's full name
   - Date of birth
   - Address
   - At least one additional data point (e.g., SIN partial match)
d) Record the name of the credit bureau, date of query, and results

DECISION: Was the identity successfully verified?
- If YES → proceed to Step 9
- If NO → proceed to Step 10

STEP 8: Dual-Process Method
-----------------------------
Owner: Compliance Officer (Human)
This method uses two independent sources from different categories:

Category A — Government-issued ID (no photo required):
- Birth certificate, marriage certificate, CRA documents

Category B — Financial records:
- Bank statement, credit card statement, mortgage documents

Category C — Utility or institutional records:
- Utility bill, insurance policy, educational transcript

a) Obtain one document from two different categories
b) Verify that the name on both documents matches the application
c) Confirm at least one additional identifier (DOB or address) appears on
   at least one document
d) Record both document types, dates, and verification details

DECISION: Was the identity successfully verified?
- If YES → proceed to Step 9
- If NO → proceed to Step 10

STEP 9: Create Compliance Record and Open Account
--------------------------------------------------
Owner: System (Automated)
Upon successful identity verification:
a) Create a verification record containing:
   - Method used for verification
   - Documents reviewed and their details
   - Name of the individual who verified identity (if applicable)
   - Date and time of verification
   - Risk rating assigned
   - Whether EDD was performed and its outcome
b) Store the record in the compliance database
c) Set the verification expiry reminder (re-verify based on risk level)
d) Open the client's account(s)
e) Send confirmation to the client

All records must be retained for at least 5 years after the business
relationship ends, as required by FINTRAC.

STEP 10: Escalate to Senior Compliance Officer
------------------------------------------------
Owner: Senior Compliance Officer (Human)
When identity verification fails or cannot be completed:
a) The case is escalated to the Senior Compliance Officer
b) Review the verification attempt details and failure reason
c) Determine whether:
   - An alternative verification method should be attempted
   - Additional documents should be requested from the client
   - The application should be declined
   - A Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) should be filed with FINTRAC
d) Document the decision and rationale
e) If declined, notify the client without disclosing that an STR may be filed
   (tipping off is prohibited under the PCMLTFA)

================================================================================
4. RECORD KEEPING
================================================================================

All verification records, screening results, risk assessments, and decisions
must be retained for a minimum of 5 years after the last transaction or the end
of the business relationship, whichever is later.
